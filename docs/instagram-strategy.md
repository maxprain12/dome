## 2. Brand Voice

| Attribute | Description |
|---|---|
| **Tone** | Smart but accessible. Talk like a knowledgeable colleague, not a manual. |
| **Persona** | Researchers, grad students, writers, intensely curious people. |
| **Primary language** | English. Spanish for LATAM-targeted content. |
| **Avoid** | Empty hyperboles ("revolutionary", "life-changing"). Forced startup jargon. |
| **Use** | Concrete use cases. Before/after. Questions the user is already asking themselves. |

### Voice examples

**BAD:** _"Dome is the ultimate tool that will revolutionize your productivity."_

**GOOD:** _"You have 47 highlighted PDFs and you can't remember what's in any of them. Dome indexes them and lets you ask questions like you're talking to a person."_

## 3. Content Pillars

### Pillar 1 — Product Demo (40%)
Screenshots, screen recordings, and GIFs showing real features.

Post ideas:
- "This is how Dome searches inside your PDFs"
- "Create flashcards from any note in one click"
- "The AI agent that summarizes your sources before you write"
- "Calendar view with your resources and notes for the day"

### Pillar 2 — Education / Workflows (30%)
Content that teaches something useful even if the user doesn't use Dome.

Post ideas:
- "5 ways to organize your research library"
- "How to take notes that still make sense 6 months later"
- "The difference between taking notes and building knowledge"
- "What is semantic search and why it changes how you find information"

### Pillar 3 — Community / Relatability (20%)
Content that generates identification and conversation.

Post ideas:
- "The feeling of finding exactly what you were looking for in your own notes 🤌"
- "How many tabs do you have open right now? (no lying)"
- "Name something more frustrating than losing an article you already read"
- Polls: "How do you organize your sources?" · "Do you use AI for research?"

### Pillar 4 — Behind the Product / Transparency (10%)
Humanizes the project. Builds trust.

Post ideas:
- "Why we decided Dome should be a desktop app, not a web app"
- "How Dome's document indexing engine works"
- "Visual changelog: what shipped this week"
- "The bug that took us 3 days to find (and what we learned)"

## 4. Formats by Content Type

| Format | Recommended use | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **Carousel (3–8 slides)** | Tutorials, educational lists, comparisons | 2–3 × week |
| **Reel (15–30 sec)** | Feature demo, full workflow, before/after | 1–2 × week |
| **Static image** | Quotes, stats, relatable phrases | 1 × week |
| **Stories** | Polls, behind the scenes, links, quick announcements | Daily |
| **Story Highlights** | Grouped by category: Demo, Tips, Updates, FAQ | Permanent |

## 6. Recommended Hashtags

### Always use (high relevance):
`#PKM` `#knowledgemanagement` `#secondbrain` `#notetaking` `#productivity`

### Rotate based on post topic:
`#research` `#students` `#gradschool` `#thesis` `#researchmethods`
`#AI` `#artificialintelligence` `#studytools` `#learning`
`#buildinpublic` `#devlog` `#changelog` `#indiedev` `#macos`

### Avoid:
Hashtags with tens of millions of generic posts (`#motivation`, `#success`, `#tech`) — they dilute reach.

**Quantity:** 5–10 hashtags per post. Don't oversaturate.
