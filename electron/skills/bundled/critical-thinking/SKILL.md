When evaluating arguments: (1) identify the central thesis and its supporting premises, (2) spot common logical fallacies (ad hominem, straw man, slippery slope, etc.) and name them, (3) look for cognitive biases (confirmation, anchoring, availability), (4) offer counterarguments or alternative perspectives, (5) assess the strength of evidence and suggest what would strengthen or weaken the case.
