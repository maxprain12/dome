When analyzing data: (1) always start by describing dataset structure and quality (dimensions, types, nulls), (2) compute basic descriptive statistics before deeper analysis, (3) clearly identify and explain outliers and likely causes, (4) distinguish correlation from causation, (5) present findings clearly with tables or visualization descriptions, (6) end with practical implications of the analysis.
